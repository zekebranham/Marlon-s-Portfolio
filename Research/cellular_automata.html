Cellular Automata: Modeling Social Mobility and Human Success

Overview

This research project explores the power of cellular automata (CA) to model complex societal behaviors through visual simulation. The model begins with traditional elementary CA and evolves through six phases into a fully featured 3-state system that simulates human generational success, social class dynamics, and economic mobility.

Key concepts include: stochastic transitions, deterministic rule sets, inherited success, systemic intervention, and context-driven outcomes based on environment, family structure, and personal drive. The result is a layered simulation that reveals how even basic rule systems can represent macro-level societal structures.

This project was the result of collaborative undergraduate research with Jonathan Garza and was completed in Spring 2025.

Research Paper

The full research paper, titled Modeling Human Success and Social Mobility Through Incremental Development of a Multi-State Cellular Automaton, details the six phases of the model and includes visualization outputs and sociological implications.
